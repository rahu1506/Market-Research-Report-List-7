<p><p>Landfill Gas-to-Energy (LFGE) involves capturing methane and other gases emitted from landfills to convert them into usable energy. The market is divided into two primary types: thermal technologies and biochemical reactions. Thermal technologies utilize high-temperature processes like combustion or gasification to turn landfill gas into electricity or heat. In contrast, biochemical reactions involve anaerobic digestion, where microorganisms break down organic waste to produce biogas, which can then be used for electricity generation or as a renewable natural gas substitute.</p></p>

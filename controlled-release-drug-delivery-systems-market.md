<p><p>Controlled-release drug delivery systems are designed to release medication at a controlled rate, enhancing therapeutic efficacy and minimizing side effects. The Wurster technique involves fluid bed coating for sustained release. Coacervation encapsulates drugs in a polymeric shell. Microencapsulation involves creating microscopic capsules for targeted release. Implants provide long-term release directly at the site of action. Transdermal systems deliver drugs through the skin for systemic effects. Targeted delivery focuses on directing drugs to specific cells or tissues. Other methods include nanoparticle systems and biodegradable polymers.</p></p>

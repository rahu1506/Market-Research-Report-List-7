<p><p>The brass tube market encompasses three primary manufacturing processes: Casting-Rolling (CR), Extrusion, and other specialized methods. In Casting-Rolling, molten brass is poured into molds and then rolled into tubes, allowing for intricate shapes and sizes. Extrusion involves forcing heated brass through a die to create uniform cross-section tubes. Each method serves distinct applications, influencing the properties, dimensions, and production efficiency of the finished products, catering to various industrial needs such as plumbing, electrical, and automotive sectors.</p></p>

<p><p>Low Iron Clear Flat Glass is categorized primarily into Rolled Glass and Float Glass. Rolled Glass is produced by rolling molten glass into sheets, resulting in a textured surface and specific applications like architectural features or decorative items. Float Glass, on the other hand, involves floating molten glass on molten tin to achieve a smooth, clear, and uniform thickness, making it ideal for high-visibility applications, such as windows and glass facades. Both types serve distinct purposes in various industries.</p></p>

<p><p>The online travel market consists of two primary types: Online Travel Agencies (OTAs) and Direct Travel Suppliers. OTAs are intermediaries that aggregate travel services, such as flights, hotels, and car rentals, offering consumers a one-stop platform for booking and comparison. In contrast, Direct Travel Suppliers include airlines, hotel chains, and tour operators that sell their offerings directly to customers, often through their own websites. Both models cater to travelers but differ in distribution and pricing strategies.</p></p>

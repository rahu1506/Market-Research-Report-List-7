<p><p>The alternative protein fermentation market encompasses three primary types: </p><p>1. **Traditional Fermentation** involves using microorganisms to ferment plant-based ingredients, enhancing flavors and nutritional properties, often seen in products like tempeh and miso.  </p><p>   </p><p>2. **Biomass Fermentation** focuses on cultivating microbial biomass, such as mycelium, to create high-protein foods resembling meat or fish.  </p><p>3. **Precision Fermentation** utilizes engineered microbes to produce specific proteins or molecules, offering tailored nutritional profiles and sustainability benefits, as seen in dairy or egg replacements.</p></p>
